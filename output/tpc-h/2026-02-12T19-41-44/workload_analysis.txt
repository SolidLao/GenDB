# Workload Analysis
# Scale Factor: 10
# Optimization Target: execution_time
# Queries: Q1, Q3, Q6 (TPC-H subset)

## Tables (Estimated Sizes at SF=10)

### Fact Tables
- lineitem: ~60M rows, 16 columns
  Hot columns: l_shipdate (all queries), l_extendedprice, l_discount, l_quantity (Q1, Q6),
               l_returnflag, l_linestatus (Q1), l_orderkey (Q3)

- orders: ~15M rows, 9 columns
  Hot columns: o_orderkey (Q3 join), o_custkey (Q3 join), o_orderdate (Q3 filter),
               o_shippriority (Q3 output)

### Dimension Tables
- customer: ~1.5M rows, 8 columns
  Hot columns: c_custkey (Q3 join), c_mktsegment (Q3 filter, ~20% selectivity)

- part: ~2M rows, 9 columns (not used in sample queries)

- supplier: ~100K rows, 7 columns (not used in sample queries)

- partsupp: ~8M rows, 5 columns (not used in sample queries)

- nation: 25 rows (tiny, not used in sample queries)

- region: 5 rows (tiny, not used in sample queries)

## Join Graph

### Q3 Joins
- customer.c_custkey = orders.o_custkey (PK-FK, 1:N)
  Selectivity: ~20% of customers (c_mktsegment filter)

- lineitem.l_orderkey = orders.o_orderkey (FK-PK, N:1)
  Selectivity: filtered by date ranges on both sides
  Estimated: ~10-15% of lineitem rows after l_shipdate filter

## Selective Filters

### Date Ranges (High Selectivity)
- l_shipdate <= '1998-09-02' (Q1): ~98% of data (very inclusive)
- l_shipdate BETWEEN '1994-01-01' and '1994-12-31' (Q6): ~12.5% selectivity (1 year of 8 years)
- l_shipdate > '1995-03-15' (Q3): ~50% selectivity
- o_orderdate < '1995-03-15' (Q3): ~40% selectivity

### Other Filters
- c_mktsegment = 'BUILDING' (Q3): ~20% selectivity (5 market segments)
- l_discount BETWEEN 0.05 AND 0.07 (Q6): ~10% selectivity (narrow range)
- l_quantity < 24 (Q6): ~48% selectivity (quantity range 1-50)

## Aggregations

### Q1: GROUP BY l_returnflag, l_linestatus
- Group keys: 2 low-cardinality columns (3-4 distinct combinations)
- Aggregates: SUM, AVG, COUNT on numeric columns
- Output: tiny result set (3-4 rows)

### Q3: GROUP BY l_orderkey, o_orderdate, o_shippriority
- Group keys: mix of unique (l_orderkey) and low-cardinality (o_shippriority)
- Estimated groups: ~10K-100K after filters
- Aggregates: SUM(l_extendedprice * (1 - l_discount))
- Output: top 10 after sort

### Q6: Single aggregate value (no GROUP BY)

## Sort/Limit Requirements

- Q1: ORDER BY l_returnflag, l_linestatus (tiny result, no limit)

## Parallelism Opportunities

### Scan Parallelism
- lineitem (60M rows): can split into multiple row groups for parallel processing
- orders (15M rows): moderate parallelism benefit
- customer (1.5M rows): limited benefit from parallelism

### Independent Operations
- Q1, Q6: embarrassingly parallel scans (no joins)
- Q3: can parallelize each table scan, then join

## Key Optimization Opportunities

### 1. Sort lineitem by l_shipdate (CRITICAL)
- Enables row group pruning for all 3 queries
- Q1: skip row groups with l_shipdate > '1998-09-02'
- Q3: skip row groups with l_shipdate <= '1995-03-15'
- Q6: only scan row groups overlapping '1994-01-01' to '1994-12-31'
- Expected speedup: 2-10x on Q6 (most selective), 1.5-2x on Q3

### 2. Sort orders by o_orderdate
- Enables row group pruning for Q3 (o_orderdate < '1995-03-15')
- Expected speedup: 1.5-2x on Q3

### 3. Dictionary Encode Low-Cardinality Strings
- lineitem: l_returnflag (3 values), l_linestatus (2 values),
            l_shipinstruct (4 values), l_shipmode (7 values)
- orders: o_orderstatus (3 values), o_orderpriority (5 values)
- customer: c_mktsegment (5 values)
- Benefits: 50-80% space reduction, faster equality checks, better cache locality

### 4. Row Group Sizing for lineitem
- Use smaller row groups (50K-100K rows) for better date range pruning
- Trade-off: slightly more metadata, but much better selectivity on Q6 (12.5% filter)

### 5. Compression Strategy
- lineitem, orders: snappy (fast decompression, moderate CPU)
- Small tables (nation, region, customer): zstd (better compression, size matters less)
- For execution_time optimization, prefer snappy over zstd

### 6. Column Pruning
- Q3 and Q6 access <25% of lineitem columns → Parquet's columnar format provides massive I/O savings
